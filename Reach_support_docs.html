All support issues must be raised via Reach Central — click Create Ticket from the portal. Direct emails to engineers are not supported unless escalating a P1 or P2 major incident.

How to raise a ticket in Reach Central

1

Log in to Reach Central

Open Reach Central using your client credentials. Contact your account manager if you need access provisioned.

2

Click "Create Ticket"

The create ticket form will open as shown on the right. Fill in all fields carefully — the more detail provided, the faster the resolution.

3

Select issue type & urgency

Choose Incident for outages and service disruptions, or the appropriate type for your request. Set Urgency to match the P1–P4 priority guide on the Overview tab.

4

Attach supporting files

Upload screenshots, error logs, or XMLs using the Attachment field. Supporting files significantly speed up resolution.

5

Click "Create" and track

Submit the form. You will receive a ticket reference number. Monitor all updates via Reach Central — all communications are logged on the ticket timeline.

Escalation process — step by step

1

Detect and classify the issue

Identify the severity using the P1–P4 guide on the Overview tab. If service is fully down or there is widespread user impact, classify immediately as P1.

2

Complete pre-troubleshooting steps

Complete the Tier 1 network troubleshooting checklist on the Response Times tab before escalating. Document all steps taken and outcomes.

3

Log a ticket in Jira immediately

Create an Incident ticket in the Reach Central portal. Set the correct priority. The SLA clock starts from ticket creation time.

4

Send escalation email for P1 / P2 major issues

Email rmts@reachmobile.com with subject: [P1] ClientName — Issue Summary — Ticket #XXXX. Include affected service, impact scope, and the ticket URL.

5

Await acknowledgement

The Technical Support team will acknowledge within the SLA response window for your client group. All further communication goes through the ticket — avoid sending duplicate emails.

6

Post-incident review (P1 only)

For all resolved P1 incidents, a root cause analysis (RCA) will be completed and shared within 48 hours of resolution. The RCA document is attached to the original ticket.
