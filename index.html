Escalation rule

Always start at level 1. Only escalate to the next level if there is no response within the SLA window for the ticket priority.

Raising a Ticket
All support issues must be raised via Reach Central. Direct emails to engineers are not supported unless escalating a P1 or P2 incident.
How to raise a ticket in Reach Central
1

Log in to Reach Central

Open Reach Central using your client credentials. Contact your account manager if you need access.

2

Click "Create Ticket"

The create ticket form will open. Fill in all fields carefully.

3

Select issue type & urgency

Choose Incident for outages. Set Urgency to match the P1–P4 priority guide.

4

Attach supporting files

Upload screenshots, error logs, or XMLs. Supporting files speed up resolution.

5

Click "Create" and track

You will receive a ticket reference number. Monitor all updates in Reach Central.

Escalation Process
Follow this process for all P1 incidents and major service disruptions.
⚠ P1 / Major incident escalation

Send an email immediately to [email protected]
Subject: [P1] ClientName — Issue Summary — Ticket #XXXX

Step-by-step escalation
1

Detect and classify

Identify severity using the P1–P4 guide. Full service down or widespread impact = P1 immediately.

2

Complete pre-troubleshooting steps

Complete the checklist on the Response Times tab. Document all steps and outcomes.

3

Log a ticket in Reach Central

Create an Incident ticket with the correct priority. The SLA clock starts from ticket creation time.

4

Send escalation email (P1 / P2)

Email [email protected] — Subject: [P1] ClientName — Issue — Ticket #XXXX. Include affected service, impact scope, and ticket reference.

5

Await acknowledgement

The team will acknowledge within the SLA response window. All further communication goes through the Reach Central ticket.

6

Post-incident review (P1 only)

A root cause analysis (RCA) will be shared within 48 hours of resolution, attached to the original ticket.
