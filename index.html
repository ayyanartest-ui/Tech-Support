Support Levels
Reach operates three levels of support. Issues are handled at the lowest level possible, escalating only when the current level is unable to resolve the issue.
Level 1 (L1) — Frontline Support Direct customer contact

L1 support is the direct communication point for customers. L1 assists customers with common queries pertaining to their mobile accounts and concerns regarding potential service issues. L1 performs initial troubleshooting for network and service related issues, and basic web and app queries. If general troubleshooting in Reach Central or on the device is unable to resolve the issue, L1 can escalate by creating a ticket to the L2 team.

When should L1 escalate to L2?

  • An action cannot be completed by L1 agents through Reach Central
  • Customers report network trouble and L1 cannot resolve the issue with tools in Reach Central and/or by performing troubleshooting steps directly on the device
  • There is an error or pop-up message in Reach Central that is not known to L1 agents
↓
Level 2 (L2) — Technical Support Escalated from L1

L2 support assists L1 with any errors seen in Reach Central alongside network and service related issues.

Queries L2 can assist with:

  • Checking network outages
  • Updating the IMEI for customers if the IMEI does not take automatically
  • Updating billing address
  • Updating shipping address
  • Checking coverage specifics for an impacted area
  • Updating ICCID number for customers if the ICCID does not take automatically
  • Checking and updating live data usage
  • Updating the email address of the customer
  • Checking the order status if unable to see from Reach Central
  • Checking on refund status with finance team
  • Disconnecting an account
  • Performing resets for data if the bill is paid and the cycle does not reset
  • Updating the active status of an account

When should L2 escalate to L3?

  • Processing any refund
  • Data syncing issue — data mismatch, data throttled, data not added
  • App login error
  • Any credits to be applied
  • Data usage for the day
  • CDR report of the data
  • Connectivity issue
  • Internet speed issue
↓
Level 3 (L3) — Engineering / Backend Escalated from L2

L3 handles issues that require backend system access, engineering intervention, or issues relating to billing, account access, and certain connectivity reporting. L3 issues are escalated via ticket from L2 and are not directly accessible by customers or L1.

Raising a Ticket
All support issues must be raised via Reach Central. Direct emails to engineers are not supported unless escalating a P1 or P2 major incident.
How to raise a ticket in Reach Central
1

Log in to Reach Central

Open Reach Central using your client credentials. Contact your account manager if you need access provisioned.

2

Click Create Ticket

The create ticket form will open. Fill in all fields carefully — more detail means faster resolution.

3

Select issue type and urgency

Choose Incident for outages and disruptions. Set Urgency to match the priority: P1 = Critical, P2 = High, P3 = Medium, P4 = Low.

4

Attach supporting files

Upload screenshots, error logs, or XMLs. Attachments significantly speed up resolution.

5

Submit and track

You will receive a ticket reference number. Monitor all updates and communications directly in Reach Central.

SLA clock rules

Response time is the time for acknowledgement of receipt of an incident alert. Restoration time is the time for restoration of Reach Service allowing full service. Resolution time is the time for total resolution including a permanent bug fix or patch being applied.

Priority & Terms
As soon as a valid support request is received, Reach reviews the details and assigns an initial priority level. This classification ensures all requests are handled consistently.
Priority level classification
PriorityClassificationDefinition
P1 Critical incident
  • Whole of or a critical part of the Reach Service is unusable, causing immediate and significant business impact
  • Actual or alleged data security breaches
  • Requires follow-up status communications by direct personal contact to the ECHO Partner at each agreed period of time via live bridge, regardless of when the incident occurs
P2 Major incident
  • A significant but not immediately critical part of the Reach Service is unusable, creating major business impact
  • Requires follow-up status communications by direct personal contact to the ECHO Partner via live bridge, regardless of when the incident occurs
P3 Minor incident
  • A non-revenue generating issue of the Reach Service is not functioning, or causing negative end user experiences
  • Disruption of a single element of the Reach Service not causing impact to normal business
P4 Non-service affecting
  • Causing inconvenience only and not interrupting services to live subscribers
  • UI issue of the Reach Services portal not impacting live user experience for conducting business
Term definitions
TermDefinition
Response time The time for acknowledgement of receipt of an incident alert from the ECHO Partner or Reach monitoring alarms
Restoration time The time for restoration of the Reach Service, allowing full service by Reach to resume
Resolution time The time for total resolution of service, where a permanent bug fix or patch has been applied
Service degradation A condition where there is a partial failure of the Reach Service to serve within the Service Level
Service failure A condition where there is a complete failure of the Reach Service to deliver any service within the Service Level to all live users
Escalation A formal indication that business urgency is required from the ECHO Partner to Reach
